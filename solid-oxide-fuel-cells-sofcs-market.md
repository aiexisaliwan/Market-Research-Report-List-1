<p><p>Solid Oxide Fuel Cells (SOFCs) are electrochemical devices that convert chemical energy from fuels into electricity with high efficiency and low emissions. The market for SOFCs is categorized into three types: planar, thin film, and tubular. Planar SOFCs feature flat, stacked cell designs, offering compactness and ease of manufacturing. Thin film SOFCs utilize a lightweight layer for enhanced power density and flexibility. Tubular SOFCs consist of cylindrical cells, providing robustness and higher thermal stability, making them suitable for various applications.</p></p>
